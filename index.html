Case Study

From paper tickets to a data-driven service platform

The problem: a private higher education institution in Basilan managed registrar, cashier, and records queues entirely by hand — shouted numbers, no wait-time visibility, and frequent routing errors during peak enrollment.

The approach: HARDAM Queue was designed around the institution's actual department structure from day one, replacing paper tickets with digital queuing, real-time TV displays, and automatic routing — deployed with zero downtime to daily operations.

The result: 52% shorter average wait times and 79% higher daily transaction capacity, with the same staff headcount. The same architecture has since been extended to 4 additional institutions without a single code rewrite.
